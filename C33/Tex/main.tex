\begin{abstract}                                                %Beginn des Abstracts
\vspace{1em}
	\noindent
	The $T^4$ dependency of the Stefan-Boltzmann law has been verified via measurements on the Leslie's cube and on a cavity which simulated a blackbody.
	The infrared transmittance of window glass, silicon and sodium chloride have been measured to $t_{glass} = (2.27 \pm 0.48)\%$, $t_{silicon} = (46.82 \pm 0.48)\%$
	and $t_{sodium chloride} = (73.32 \pm 0.48)\%$ respectively. Further the angle dependency of the Lambert's law for a cavity radiator at $T = 371^\circ$C
	has been tested and validated. 
\end{abstract}
