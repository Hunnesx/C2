\section{Introduction}

Every object with a temperature above absolut zero emits electromagnetic waves in the form of thermal radiation. In an thermodynamical equilibrium
this radiation is described by the Stefan-Boltzmann law. \\ 
Generally it is important in many field how different materials absorb or emit radiation - for example in construction it is necessary for insulation so that
as little thermal radiation is absorbed. The respective abilities of the materials used depend heavily on their properties and internal
structure - for effective application, therefore, knowledge of the material-specific properties is important. \\
In the following those characteristics for different surfaces will be examined.
