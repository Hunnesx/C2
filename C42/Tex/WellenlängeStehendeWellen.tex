\section{Determination of the wavelength via standing waves}
\noindent
\setlength{\parindent}{0pt}

The wavelength of the microwave radiation was determined using standing waves. For this purpose, 
the positions of several nodes were measured along the optical bench. Since the distance between 
two neighboring nodes corresponds to half the wavelength, the wavelength can be calculated using

\begin{equation}
    \lambda = 2 {\Delta x},
\end{equation}

where ${\Delta x}$ is the mean distance between adjacent nodes.

From the measured values, an average node spacing of

\begin{equation}
    {\Delta x}
    =
    (1.628 \pm 0.026)cm
\end{equation}

was obtained. This results in a wavelength of

\begin{equation}
    \lambda
    =
    (3.256 \pm 0.052) cm.
\end{equation}

The experimentally determined wavelength is in good agreement with the expected wavelength of 
the microwave source.
