\begin{abstract}                                                %Beginn des Abstracts
\vspace{1em}
	\noindent

In this experiment, several fundamental properties of microwave radiation were investigated using a Gunn diode as 
a microwave source. The wavelength of the microwaves was determined using standing waves and a Michelson interferometer, 
yielding
$\lambda = (3.256 \pm 0.052)cm$ and
$\lambda = (3.300 \pm 0.100)cm$, respectively.
\noindent
Furthermore, the angular distribution and polarization properties of the radiation were examined. The polarization 
measurements confirmed that the emitted microwave radiation is linearly polarized.
\noindent
Frustrated total reflection was investigated using two paraffin prisms separated by an air gap. The transmitted 
intensity showed an exponential decrease with increasing prism separation, resulting in a penetration depth of
$\delta = (2.31 \pm 0.14)cm$.
\noindent
In addition, Bragg diffraction on a macroscopic cubic crystal was used to determine the lattice constant, yielding 
values of

$d_0 = (3.74 \pm 0.14)cm$ and
$d_0 = (4.03 \pm 0.10)cm$ for different lattice planes.
\noindent
Overall, the experiment demonstrated several characteristic wave phenomena of microwave radiation and illustrated 
their relevance for optics, solid state physics, and quantum mechanics.


\end{abstract}
