\section{Determination of the wavelength via the Michelson interferometer}
\noindent
\setlength{\parindent}{0pt}

The wavelength of the microwave radiation was additionally determined using a Michelson interferometer. By changing the position of one of the mirrors, the optical path difference between the two partial beams changes, resulting in alternating constructive and destructive interference.

The wavelength can be determined from the average distance between neighboring interference maxima according to

\begin{equation}
    \lambda = 2 \Delta x,
\end{equation}

where $\Delta x$ denotes the mean distance between adjacent maxima.

From the measured values, an average distance of

\begin{equation}
    {\Delta x}
    =
    (1.650 \pm 0.050)cm
\end{equation}

was obtained. This results in a wavelength of

\begin{equation}
    \lambda
    =
    (3.300 \pm 0.100)cm.
\end{equation}

The experimentally determined wavelength agrees within its margin of error with the wavelength obtained from the standing wave measurement.
