\begin{abstract}                                                %Beginn des Abstracts
\vspace{1em}
	\noindent
ABSTRACT

In this experiment, the Faraday effect in an N-SF6 glass sample was investigated by measuring the rotation of the 
plane of polarisation of linearly polarised light in an external magnetic field. The effective magnetic flux density 
inside the solenoid was determined from the measured magnetic field profile using a Hall probe, since the field 
distribution was not perfectly homogeneous.

The Faraday rotation angle was measured for three different wavelength ranges between 360,nm and 630,nm as a function 
of the magnetic flux density. For all investigated wavelength ranges, an approximately linear dependence between the 
rotation angle and the magnetic field was observed. Linear regressions were used to determine the slopes of the measured 
data, from which the corresponding Verdet constants were calculated.

The measured Verdet constants were found to decrease from $(23.37 \pm 0.40),\mathrm{rad(Tm)^{-1}}$ at $\lambda = 410,\mathrm{nm}$ 
to $(13.62 \pm 0.24),\mathrm{rad(Tm)^{-1}}$ at $\lambda = 595,\mathrm{nm}$. This behaviour is consistent with the expected 
wavelength dependence of dispersive optical media, where shorter wavelengths experience a stronger rotation of the plane 
of polarisation than longer wavelengths.

Overall, the experiment successfully confirmed both the linear dependence of the Faraday rotation on the magnetic flux 
density and the dispersion of the Verdet constant in N-SF6 glass.

\end{abstract}
